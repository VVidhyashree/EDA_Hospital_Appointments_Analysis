These findings highlight the counter-intuitive relationships between specific conditions and appointment attendance:

#Hypertension,Patients with Hypertension (85% Show Rate) are significantly more likely to attend than those without (78% Show Rate).,A diagnosed chronic condition appears to drive higher compliance.
#Diabetes,Patients with Diabetes (83% Show Rate) are slightly more likely to attend than those without (80% Show Rate).,"Similar to Hypertension, a chronic diagnosis correlates with better attendance."
#Scholarship,Patients without a Scholarship (80% Show Rate) have a slightly higher attendance rate than those with a Scholarship (75% Show Rate).,Financial aid status does not guarantee higher attendance; other factors may outweigh the benefit.
